Camping



Love Camping? So do We... Whether you’re looking for back-to-basics beauty, a fun family getaway, or a romantic retreat, we’ve got you covered with our picks of campsites in the Cape. Pitch your tent beside the sea, under a tree or upon a hill, fall asleep to the sounds of nature. Some of the well known camp sites in the Western Cape include Secret falls in Winterhoek Mountains, Nature's valley rest camp in Garden Route and Badensfontein camping thats in Montague
Important note: Its important to always keep your hygiene, always carry a survival kit and prepare yourself just in case you get lost.

Hiking



Whether you’re after a windswept coastal wander, a mountainous hike with spectacular views, or a leisurely amble through one of the Cape’s lush nature reserves, here’s our pick of the best hiking trails in and around the city. So, lace up your hiking boots and get cracking. Famous hiking trails include Devil's Peak, Lion's Head hike and Skeleton gorge, there's also and exciting hiking trail up table mountain with two paths, one for beginners and another for the more experienced group of people
Important note: Although the Cape is rich in natural beauty, tourists and locals are urged to take necessary precautions when exploring secluded areas, as crimes and accidents do happen.

Fishing



If you looking for big game fish you should check in and around False Bay and the Cape Peninsula where you are likely to catch bluefin and yellowfin Tuna. In Rooikrans you are likely to catch Blacktail, Hottentot, Kob (Kabeljou), Galjoen off the cliff ledges. Rooikrans is located on the Cape of Good Hope section of the Table Mountain National Park. In Kommetjie Bay expect the Cape snoek run in the winter season and you can catch white steenbras, stumpnose & galjoen off the rocks at Kommetjie. If you looking for fresh water fish then the Fischerhaven Dam is located just outside of Hermans and is great for bass, rainbow trout fishing and carp
Important note: Recreational Fishing is regulated by “bag limits” and all fishers are required to get recreational fishing permits. There is also a 10 bag fishing limit

Rock Climbing



The Cape is full of mountains and provides a mecca of climbing. Cape Town being the main center is a good base to start from. There are a few clubs mainly the MCSA Cape Town Section which has over 1200 members and a bouldering wall in their club room. Good climbing is only a few minutes drive from Cape Town and the furthermost areas about 4-5 hours away. There are hundreds of crags outside of the city at Du Toit's Kloof, Montagu, Paarl, the Cederberg as well as other locations. There are also great bouldering opportunities in Rocklands.. Beginners are always welcomed. Instructors will show you the basics, teach you about safety and get you onto rock for your first rock climbing experience.
Important note:Cape-Climbers refer to as the "Schlauk Factor"-the rock becomes so slippery and even damp that it seems as if someone smeared oil onto the holds. Climbing is near impossible unless you have a bucket of chalk to compensate! Always climb in groups, try not to climb alone!
